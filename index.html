Interests & Focus Areas

Systems, Performance & Low-Level Engineering

I am deeply interested in understanding how software actually works beneath the abstractions. This includes computer architecture, operating systems, and low-level performance behavior. I enjoy exploring topics like memory models, threading, race conditions, synchronization, cache behavior, and scheduling, and how these influence real-world system performance.

I have a strong interest in modern C++, particularly areas such as template metaprogramming, compile-time techniques, performance-oriented design, and writing predictable, efficient code. I like studying how compilers transform code and how small implementation choices can have significant performance implications.

Algorithms, Mathematics & Problem Solving

I am strongly drawn to algorithmic thinking and mathematical problem solving. I enjoy working on topics such as algorithms and data structures, game theory and strategic reasoning, probability and combinatorics, advanced dynamic programming patterns, invariants and constructive techniques, and quantitative logic puzzles.

Competitive programming is especially appealing to me because it sits at the intersection of theory, implementation, and performance under constraints.

Quantitative Thinking & Puzzles

I am fascinated by quantitative reasoning, especially problems that require deep insight rather than formulaic approaches. I enjoy solving quant puzzles, logical riddles, and problems that demand careful modeling and precise reasoning.
